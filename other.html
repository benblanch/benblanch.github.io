Robin's Eye

Looking down on the bustling street below,
a robin sits atop a telephone line.
A passing car coughs exhaust backwards.
Disturbed by the unnatural air the Robin looses a high pitched EEK.
It nearly leaves the comfortable perch to search for another,
but doesn’t bother.

Below, the people never stop.
They are always doing something,
but also nothing at the same time.
One shouts on a phone.
Another runs across the street
causing a tire SCREECH and HONK from a truck.
A pair exits a nearby store weighed down by heavy bags.

The Robin is confused every time it stops to watch.
Where are they going?
What are they saying?
Is there something they are chasing?
It will never have an answer.
The humans will never stop.
They don’t realize that they are flying into the wind.
They can’t see through the Robin’s eye.

The Robin looks to the sky and its heart rate returns to rest.
It closes its eyes.
A gentle breeze flows through its feathers.
It flys along.

Valley

There’s a sunken place
Where a stream trickles down as it’s fed by dropping clouds.
It is surrounded by walls that are enveloped with life.
They protect the sunken place from everything.
They rise to the sky
where the misty crown of cloud shields their peaks from invasive eyes.
Beyond the sunken place there is a dry expanse that is met by the ocean.
A blessing and curse.
A barrier that encroaches without invitation.
Biding its time to swallow the sunken place.
Beyond the ocean is humanity.
Who fuels the oceans and their conquest.
They surge forward.

Sure some try,
but the rest shift their gaze away from the last place on earth.
That sinking sunken place.
